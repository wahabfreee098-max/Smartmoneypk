How to pay off debt faster in Pakistan — two proven strategies

The snowball and avalanche methods, explained simply with local examples.

📅 June 2026⏱ 6 min read✍️ SmartMoney PK Team

Whether it's a credit card balance, personal loan, or amount owed to family, carrying debt creates ongoing financial stress. Two well-established strategies can help you pay it off systematically rather than just making minimum payments indefinitely.

Strategy 1: The debt snowball method

List all your debts from smallest to largest balance, regardless of interest rate. Pay minimum payments on everything except the smallest debt, and put any extra money toward that smallest balance until it's paid off completely. Then move to the next smallest, rolling the freed-up payment into it.

💡 Why this works psychologically

Paying off your first debt completely, even a small one, creates a sense of momentum and progress that keeps many people motivated to continue — this matters more than people expect.

Strategy 2: The debt avalanche method

List your debts from highest interest rate to lowest, regardless of balance size. Pay minimums on everything except the highest-interest debt, and direct extra payments there first. This method saves more money overall in interest charges compared to the snowball method.

Which method should you choose?

The avalanche method is mathematically optimal — you'll pay less total interest. But the snowball method's quick wins keep many people more consistently motivated. The "best" method is genuinely the one you'll actually stick with long-term.

Before aggressive repayment — keep a small buffer

Even while paying down debt intensely, keep a small emergency buffer (even Rs 10,000-20,000) so an unexpected expense doesn't force you to take on new debt to cover it — which would undo your progress.

Tackling high-cost debt specifically

Credit card debt and certain personal loans in Pakistan can carry high effective interest rates. If you're carrying a credit card balance month to month, prioritising this debt is usually the right call — the interest cost typically outweighs benefits from other financial goals until it's cleared.

Negotiating with lenders

If you're struggling, contact your bank or lender directly — many offer restructuring options, particularly for long-standing customers. It's almost always better for both sides to negotiate a manageable repayment plan than for you to default entirely.

Avoiding new debt while paying off old debt

  • Avoid using credit cards for new purchases while paying down a balance — pay with cash or debit instead.
  • Build the habit of asking "do I need this now, or can it wait until I've saved for it?" before any non-essential purchase.
  • Track your progress monthly — seeing the balance shrink reinforces the habit.

What debt-free feels like

Once debts are cleared, redirect what you were paying toward debt into your emergency fund and investments instead — the same monthly discipline that paid off debt can now build real wealth.
