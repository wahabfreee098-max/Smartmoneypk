How to file income tax in Pakistan — a simple guide for beginners

Understanding your tax obligations and how to file without stress.

📅 June 2026⏱ 7 min read✍️ SmartMoney PK Team

Tax filing intimidates many Pakistanis, but becoming a filer (rather than a non-filer) carries real financial benefits — lower withholding tax rates on banking transactions, property purchases, and vehicle registration, among others.

Who needs to file a tax return?

Generally, anyone earning above the taxable threshold set by the FBR (Federal Board of Revenue) each year is required to file. This includes salaried individuals, freelancers, and business owners. Even if your income is below the threshold, becoming a filer often still saves money through reduced withholding tax rates.

💡 Filer vs non-filer matters more than people realise

Non-filers pay significantly higher withholding tax on bank transactions, property transfers, and vehicle purchases. The cost of staying a non-filer often exceeds any effort saved by not filing.

Step 1: Register for an NTN (National Tax Number)

This is done through the FBR's IRIS portal (iris.fbr.gov.pk) using your CNIC. Registration is free and can be completed online without visiting a tax office.

Step 2: Gather your documents

  • Salary certificate or income statement from your employer
  • Bank statements for the tax year
  • Records of any other income (freelance, rental, investment gains)
  • Records of any deductible expenses or zakat paid

Step 3: File through the IRIS portal

The FBR's IRIS system allows individuals to file returns directly online. For straightforward salaried income, the process is relatively simple. For freelance or business income, or if you find the forms confusing, a tax consultant can help — many charge reasonable fees for individual returns.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Missing the filing deadline (typically announced by FBR each year) — leads to penalties.
  • Not declaring all income sources, including freelance or rental income.
  • Forgetting to claim legitimate deductions you're entitled to, like certain investments or zakat.

What happens after filing?

Once your return is processed, you appear on the FBR's Active Taxpayer List (ATL), confirming your filer status. This status is checked automatically by banks and other institutions when calculating withholding tax rates on your transactions.

Should you hire a consultant?

If your income comes only from one salaried job with no other income sources, filing yourself through IRIS is usually manageable. If you have freelance income, multiple income sources, or investments, a tax consultant's fee is often worth it to ensure accuracy and to claim all eligible deductions.
