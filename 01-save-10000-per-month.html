10 ways to save Rs 10,000 every month without feeling broke

Practical, no-fluff tips that actually work for Pakistani families — starting this week.

📅 June 2026 ⏱ 5 min read 👁 4,200+ views ✍️ SmartMoney PK Team

Saving money in Pakistan feels harder every year. Inflation is up, utility bills keep rising, and salaries haven't kept pace. But here's the truth: most Pakistani families waste Rs 8,000–15,000 every month on small, avoidable expenses — without even realising it.

In this article, we'll show you exactly where that money goes and how to take it back — without giving up things you love.

💡 Quick tip before you start

Track your spending for just one week using your phone's Notes app. Write down every rupee you spend. Most people are shocked by what they find.

The 10 saving tips

1

Switch to a prepaid mobile plan

Most Pakistanis overspend on mobile packages they don't fully use. Switch to a weekly or monthly package that matches your actual usage. Compare Jazz, Zong, Ufone, and Telenor bundles on their apps — you can often cut your mobile bill by 40%.

💾 Save: Rs 500–1,500/month
2

Cook at home 5 days a week

Eating out at a dhaba or ordering delivery even 3–4 times a week adds up fast. A home-cooked meal costs Rs 150–300 per person vs Rs 500–1,200 eating out. Plan a simple weekly menu on Sunday and shop once — you'll save time and money.

💾 Save: Rs 2,000–4,000/month
3

Cut your electricity bill with one habit

Switch off appliances at the plug — not just standby mode. Fridges, ACs, and water geysers are the biggest culprits. Set your AC to 26°C (not 18°C) — it uses 30% less electricity. Use a smart meter or check WAPDA's calculator to identify your biggest consumers.

💾 Save: Rs 500–2,000/month
4

Shop groceries at wholesale markets

Shah Alam Market in Lahore, Empress Market in Karachi, and similar wholesale bazaars sell the same products at 20–40% less than supermarkets. Go once a month for dry goods (rice, lentils, oil, flour) and you'll notice the difference immediately.

💾 Save: Rs 1,500–3,000/month
5

Cancel unused subscriptions

Check your bank statements right now. Most Pakistanis pay for Netflix, Spotify, YouTube Premium, or app subscriptions they barely use. Share a family plan (Netflix allows up to 4 users) to split costs, or cancel and use free alternatives like YouTube's free tier.

💾 Save: Rs 300–1,200/month

6

Use EasyPaisa or JazzCash for bill payments

Paying bills through mobile wallets often has cashback offers and avoids late fees from paying in person. Set up auto-pay for WAPDA, gas, and internet bills. You'll never pay a late fee again — and the cashback adds up.

💾 Save: Rs 200–500/month in fees
7

Buy clothes and household items off-season

Winter clothes are cheapest in March. Summer clothes are cheapest in September. Eid sales offer 30–50% off. Plan ahead and you'll spend half as much on clothing for the whole family. Liberty and Packages Mall sales are particularly good for household goods.

💾 Save: Rs 500–2,000/month averaged
8

Use the 24-hour rule for non-essential purchases

Before any purchase above Rs 1,000 that wasn't planned, wait 24 hours. If you still want it after a day, buy it. Studies show this simple rule eliminates 30–40% of impulse purchases. Your future self will thank you.

💾 Save: Rs 1,000–3,000/month
9

Refinance or renegotiate any loans

If you have a car loan or personal loan from a bank, call them and ask about restructuring at a lower rate. Many people don't know you can negotiate. SBP has reduced rates — your bank may offer you a better deal rather than lose you as a customer.

💾 Save: Rs 500–5,000/month on interest
10

Automate your savings on payday

The most powerful tip: set up a separate savings account and transfer 10–20% of your salary the moment you get paid. Use Meezan Bank's profit account or a UBL digital account. What you don't see, you don't spend. This one habit builds real wealth over time.

💾 Save: Rs 5,000–20,000/month automatically

What to do with your savings

Once you're saving Rs 10,000 a month, put it to work. Here are the best options for Pakistanis in 2026:

  • Emergency fund first: Save 3 months of expenses in a high-yield savings account before investing anything.
  • Meezan Bank profit account: Currently offering some of the best returns on savings in Pakistan with Shariah compliance.
  • National Savings Certificates: Government-backed, safe, and offering 15–20% annual returns.
  • PSX mutual funds: For long-term wealth building. UBL Fund Managers and Meezan Islamic Fund are solid starting points.

Final thought

Saving Rs 10,000 a month won't happen by accident. You need to decide to do it, then set up systems so it happens automatically. Start with just 3 tips from this list today. Once those feel easy, add the rest.

Your future — the one where you're not worried about money every month — starts with one small decision right now.
