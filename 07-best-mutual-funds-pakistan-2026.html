Best mutual funds in Pakistan for 2026 (beginner-friendly guide)

How to choose a mutual fund without needing to be a finance expert.

📅 June 2026⏱ 7 min read✍️ SmartMoney PK Team

Mutual funds are one of the easiest ways for beginners to invest in Pakistan without needing deep stock market knowledge. A professional fund manager makes the investment decisions; you simply choose which fund matches your goals.

Types of mutual funds available in Pakistan

  • Equity funds: Invest mainly in stocks. Higher potential return, higher risk, best for long-term goals (5+ years).
  • Income funds: Invest in government and corporate bonds. Lower risk, steadier (but smaller) returns.
  • Money market funds: Very low risk, similar to a high-yield savings account, good for short-term parking of cash.
  • Islamic funds: Shariah-compliant versions of the above, avoiding interest-based instruments.

Major fund managers in Pakistan

UBL Fund Managers, Meezan Bank's Al Meezan Investments, NBP Fund Management, and HBL Asset Management are among the largest and most established players, each offering multiple fund types.

💡 How to start

Most fund managers let you open an account online with your CNIC and a small minimum investment, often as low as Rs 5,000-10,000.

What fees should you watch for?

Look at the fund's expense ratio (the annual fee charged regardless of performance) and any front-end load (a fee charged when you invest). Lower fees mean more of your money stays invested and compounding over time.

Matching funds to your goals

If you're saving for something within 1-2 years (a wedding, a car), a money market or income fund is more appropriate — you don't want stock market volatility right before you need the cash. If you're investing for retirement 15+ years away, an equity fund has more time to recover from short-term dips.

A simple beginner strategy

Many first-time investors split their money: a portion in a money market fund for stability, and a portion in an equity fund for growth. As you become more comfortable and your time horizon becomes clearer, you can adjust this split.

Important reminder

All mutual fund investments carry risk, and past performance doesn't guarantee future results. Read the fund's official documents (offering document and fund manager report) before investing, and consider speaking with a licensed financial advisor for guidance specific to your situation.
