Is dropshipping still profitable in Pakistan in 2026?

An honest look at the opportunities and real challenges for Pakistani dropshippers.

📅 June 2026⏱ 7 min read✍️ SmartMoney PK Team

Dropshipping — selling products online without holding inventory yourself — has been heavily promoted on social media as an easy path to income. The reality for Pakistani sellers is more nuanced. Here's an honest breakdown.

How dropshipping actually works

You list products on your own online store (or marketplace like Daraz), and when a customer orders, you purchase the item from a supplier who ships it directly to the customer. You profit from the markup between your selling price and the supplier's cost.

The real challenges in Pakistan specifically

  • Payment processing: International dropshipping platforms often require payment gateways that aren't fully available in Pakistan, complicating store setup.
  • Shipping times: If sourcing from international suppliers, shipping to your own customers can take weeks, leading to complaints and refund requests.
  • Thin margins: After advertising costs, platform fees, and supplier costs, margins are often slimmer than influencers suggest.
💡 A more realistic local approach

Many successful Pakistani sellers source products domestically (from local wholesalers in Karachi, Lahore, or Faisalabad) and sell on Daraz or their own Instagram/Facebook page — this avoids international shipping delays entirely.

Selling on Daraz as an alternative

Daraz's seller program lets you list products without necessarily holding large inventory, and benefits from Daraz's existing customer trust and payment infrastructure — often an easier starting point than building an independent store from scratch.

What it actually costs to start

Budget for: a small initial inventory test order (even with domestic sourcing, test products help you verify quality), basic product photography, and a modest advertising budget to test demand (Rs 5,000-15,000 is a reasonable test budget before scaling).

Realistic income expectations

Income from e-commerce in Pakistan varies enormously based on product choice, marketing skill, and execution. Treat any specific dollar figure promised online with healthy skepticism — many successful sellers spent months testing products before finding one that worked, and many test products fail to sell at all.

Questions to ask before starting

  • Have I validated that people actually want this product, before investing in inventory or ads?
  • Can I handle customer service and returns professionally?
  • Am I prepared for the first few months to be a learning period rather than a profit period?
