Should you invest in gold in Pakistan? A complete 2026 guide

Physical gold vs digital gold — pros, cons, and how to get started.

📅 June 2026⏱ 6 min read✍️ SmartMoney PK Team

Gold has been a trusted store of value in Pakistani households for generations — often passed down for weddings and emergencies. But is it actually a good investment in 2026, and what's the smartest way to hold it?

Why Pakistanis traditionally trust gold

Gold tends to hold its value during periods of high inflation and currency depreciation — both of which Pakistan has experienced repeatedly. Unlike the rupee, gold's value isn't tied to any single government's policy decisions.

Option 1: Physical gold (jewellery and bars)

The traditional choice. Pros: tangible, culturally significant, easy to understand. Cons: making charges on jewellery (often 15-25%) eat into returns, storage and theft risk, and it's harder to sell small amounts quickly.

💡 Tip for buying physical gold

If buying for investment (not jewellery), buy gold bars or coins rather than jewellery — you avoid the making charges entirely and get closer to the true market price.

Option 2: Digital gold

Several Pakistani fintech apps now let you buy fractional digital gold, backed by real gold stored in secure vaults. You can buy as little as Rs 500 worth. Pros: no storage risk, easy to buy and sell, no making charges. Cons: relies on the platform's credibility — research thoroughly before depositing money.

Option 3: Gold-backed mutual funds

Some asset management companies in Pakistan offer funds that track gold prices. These combine the ease of stock market investing with gold exposure, and are regulated by the SECP.

How much of your savings should be in gold?

Most financial advisors suggest gold as 5-15% of a diversified portfolio — not your entire savings. It works best as a hedge against inflation and currency risk, alongside other investments like mutual funds, savings certificates, or property.

What affects gold prices in Pakistan?

  • International gold prices (in USD)
  • The PKR/USD exchange rate — a weaker rupee makes gold more expensive locally even if international prices stay flat
  • Local demand, especially around wedding season

The bottom line

Gold is a reasonable part of a balanced portfolio in Pakistan, particularly as protection against rupee depreciation. But don't treat it as your only investment — combine it with mutual funds, savings certificates, and an emergency fund for a more complete financial plan.
