Best savings accounts in Pakistan in 2026 — where to actually keep your money

Comparing profit rates and features across major Pakistani banks.

📅 June 2026⏱ 6 min read✍️ SmartMoney PK Team

Where you keep your savings matters more than most people realise — the difference between a basic current account (which often earns nothing) and a proper savings or profit account can add up to meaningful money over time.

Conventional vs Islamic savings accounts

Pakistan offers both conventional interest-based savings accounts and Shariah-compliant Islamic accounts (often called "profit and loss sharing" accounts) from banks like Meezan Bank, BankIslami, and Islamic windows of conventional banks. Both can offer competitive returns — the difference is the underlying structure, not necessarily the rate.

What to actually compare

  • Profit/interest rate: Rates change with the State Bank of Pakistan's policy rate — compare current rates directly on each bank's website rather than relying on outdated information.
  • Minimum balance requirements: Some accounts charge fees or reduce your profit rate if your balance drops below a threshold.
  • Withdrawal limits: Some high-yield accounts limit how often you can withdraw without penalty.
  • Digital access: A strong mobile app matters for managing your account day-to-day.
💡 Don't just default to your salary bank

Many people keep savings in the same bank that processes their salary purely out of convenience — but that bank may not offer the most competitive savings rate. It's simple to open a separate savings account elsewhere while keeping your salary account unchanged.

National Savings Schemes — a strong alternative

Beyond commercial banks, the government's National Savings Centre offers certificates (like Defence Savings Certificates and Regular Income Certificates) that are backed by the government and have historically offered competitive, often higher, returns than typical bank savings accounts. These suit money you don't need immediate daily access to.

A simple savings structure to consider

  • Daily spending money: Regular current account or digital wallet.
  • Emergency fund: A savings account with easy access and a reasonable profit rate.
  • Medium-term savings (1-3 years): National Savings certificates or income funds.
  • Long-term wealth building: Mutual funds, gold, or other growth-focused investments.

Before you open any account

Check current rates directly on the State Bank of Pakistan's website or individual bank websites, since rates change with monetary policy decisions throughout the year. What was the best rate six months ago may not be the best rate today.
